7 Spectacular Space Images From August

   Katrina Miller
   Katrina MillerReporting on space

   The James Webb Space Telescope captured intricate views of the Ring
   Nebula, composed of bright halos of hydrogen around a dying star.

   Here are more images and videos from August →
   ESA/Webb, NASA, CSA, M. Barlow (UCL), N. Cox (ACRI-ST), R. Wesson
   (Cardiff University)

   The Perseids meteor shower, visible every summer in the northern
   hemisphere, peaked on Saturday, Aug 12 and Sunday, Aug. 13. These
   fireballs were captured over Herrnleis, Austria.
   Christian Bruna/EPA, via Shutterstock

   Virgin Galactic officially joined the space tourism business. On Aug.
   10, the company flew the first Olympian, Jon Goodwin, and the first
   mother-daughter duo, Keisha Schahaff and Anastatia Mayers, to the
   thermosphere and back. It’s the first majority female spaceflight.
   Virgin Galactic

   The Hubble Space Telescope snapped this image of a lenticular, or
   lens-shaped, galaxy 44 million light-years away. This ghostly star
   system was observed during a cosmic census of every known galaxy in the
   nearby universe.
   ESA/Hubble & NASA, R. Tully

   India’s moon mission includes two robots, the Pragyan rover and the
   Vikram lander. On Aug. 30, the Indian Space Research Organization
   shared a photo by Pragyan of Vikram on the lunar surface, with the
   caption, “Smile, please!”
   Indian Space Research Organization

   With a successful engine test of its Super Heavy Booster 9 rocket on
   Aug. 25, SpaceX came one step closer to sending Starship, a gigantic
   reusable spacecraft, into orbit. “Next Starship launch soon,” Elon Musk
   wrote in a post on X, formerly known as Twitter.
   SpaceX

   A rare blue supermoon, shown here over Serangan Port in Bali,
   Indonesia, graced the sky on the evening of Aug. 30.
   Made Nagi/EPA, via Shutterstock
