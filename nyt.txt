   The No-Bid Contract That Is Turning Washington’s Reflecting Pool Blue

   7 min read
   [56]David A. Fahrenthold

   David A. Fahrenthold

   The Lincoln Memorial’s reflecting pool is an icon, the unchanging
   backdrop to generations of concerts and protests. Now, Trump is
   changing it, by painting its bottom “American flag blue.”
   [57]David A. Fahrenthold

   David A. Fahrenthold

   The paint job also symbolizes something else: how Trump is bypassing
   decades-old regulations to remake Washington, and to hand out no-bid
   contracts to his preferred firms.
   [58]David A. Fahrenthold

   David A. Fahrenthold

   The contract for the work was given to a firm that Trump said had
   worked on the pool at his golf club in Northern Virginia. The Trump
   administration said it had no time to seek out other bidders: any delay
   would bring “serious injury” to the government.
   [59]David A. Fahrenthold

   David A. Fahrenthold

   Trump’s administration also did not submit the change to a commission
   that reviews changes to Washington’s monuments. As a result, experts
   say, it’s unclear if the new paint job will enhance the pool’s look —
   or seem jarringly artificial.
   [60]David A. Fahrenthold

   David A. Fahrenthold

   One other worry: algae could quickly obscure Trump’s new paint job. His
   repairs are not tackling a plumbing problem that hampers the pool’s
   filtration, leading to ugly green blooms.
