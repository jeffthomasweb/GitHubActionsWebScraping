   Nov. 25, 2023, 10:53 p.m. ET

Second Hostage-Prisoner Exchange Is Completed After a Delay

   In all, 17 hostages — 13 Israelis and four Thai nationals — arrived in
   Israel. Israel’s prison service said 39 Palestinian detainees were
   released.


   [551]

Here’s what we know about the Israeli hostages released on Saturday.

   5 min read

    1. Palestinian prisoners who had just been released by Israel arriving
       early Sunday in Ramallah, in the Israeli-occupied West Bank.
       Ammar Awad/Reuters
    2. A Red Cross vehicle, part of a convoy believed to be carrying
       hostages abducted by Hamas, arrives at the Rafah border amid an
       exchange of hostages and prisoners on Saturday.
       Fatima Shbair/Associated Press
    3. After a successful exchange of hostages and prisoners on Friday,
       the second batch was delayed on Saturday but was carried out by
       Sunday morning.
       Amit Elkayam for The New York Times
    4. Trucks loaded with humanitarian aid waiting to cross into northern
       Gaza on Saturday. The United Nations said the arrival of food,
       drinking water and medicines on Friday had allowed it to ramp up
       the scale of aid delivery, but that the enormous needs on the
       ground remained overwhelming.
       Mohammed Saber/EPA, via Shutterstock
    5. Palestinians on Saturday carried their belongings through the
       rubble of the Shati refugee camp in Gaza City.
       Associated Press
    6. The humanitarian situation has remained dire in Gaza after seven
       weeks of an Israeli blockade.
       Samar Abu Elouf for The New York Times
    7. Palestinians collected bags of flour from an aid distribution site
       in Rafah, Gaza, on Saturday.
       Associated Press
    8. Over the four-day truce that took effect at 7 a.m. local time on
       Friday, around 50 hostages are expected to be released by Hamas in
       exchange for some 150 Palestinians in Israeli custody.
